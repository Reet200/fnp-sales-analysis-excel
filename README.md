# FNP Sales Analysis (Excel Dashboard Project)

## Overview
This project analyzes sales data from a gifting business to understand revenue patterns, product performance, and customer behavior.

Instead of focusing only on visualization, the goal was to extract insights that can influence business decisions.

---

## Business Questions
The analysis focuses on:

- What drives revenue across the year?
- Which products generate the most value?
- How does customer spending vary?
- Are delivery timelines affected by order volume?
- Which cities and occasions contribute the most?

---

## Dataset Description
The project is based on three datasets:

- Customers: customer-level information  
- Orders: transaction and delivery details  
- Products: product categories and pricing  

---

## Key Metrics
- Total Revenue: ₹35.2L  
- Total Orders: 1000  
- Average Order Value: ₹3520  
- Average Delivery Time: 5.53 days  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Revenue is event-driven, not stable
Sales spike during specific months, indicating strong dependence on occasions rather than consistent demand.

### 2. High-value products dominate revenue
Bundled and premium products outperform individual items, suggesting customers prioritize convenience and perceived value.

### 3. Revenue concentration across categories
A few product categories generate most of the revenue, while others underperform.

### 4. City-level imbalance
Order volume is concentrated in a limited number of cities, indicating localized demand or operational advantages.

### 5. Occasion performance gap
Not all major occasions perform equally — some generate strong revenue, while others underperform expectations.

### 6. Delivery performance is stable
Average delivery time remains consistent, even with fluctuations in order volume.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Focus marketing on high-performing occasions  
- Improve strategy for underperforming events  
- Promote bundled/premium products more aggressively  
- Optimize operations in high-demand cities  
- Reassess low-performing product categories
